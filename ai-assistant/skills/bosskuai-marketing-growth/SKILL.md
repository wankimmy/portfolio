# BosskuAI Marketing and Growth

Use this skill when the task is about **getting attention, demand, adoption, or distribution** — turning a product into something people find, try, and recommend. Includes social content calendar planning.

## How this differs from nearby skills

- **`bosskuai-market-analysis`**: produces the market intelligence that informs this strategy; load first if positioning is unclear.
- **`bosskuai-sales-strategy`**: converts marketing-generated demand into closed deals; load alongside for full GTM coverage.
- **`bosskuai-launch-commercialization`**: orchestrates the full launch across engineering, marketing, and sales; this skill supplies the marketing component.
- **`bosskuai-paid-acquisition-monetization`**: paid channels and monetization specifically; load alongside when the growth strategy includes paid spend.
- **`bosskuai-seo-geo`**: organic discoverability through search and generative engines; load alongside for content and SEO strategy.

## Mindset

- Distribution is a product feature. Build it in, don't bolt it on after launch.
- Retention before acquisition: a leaky bucket cannot be filled with more water.
- The riskiest growth assumption is "if we build it, they will come."
- Channels have stages — what works at zero users fails at 1000, and what works at 1000 fails at 100k.
- Social media content is not about posting — it is about building an audience that trusts you enough to buy.
- Consistency beats perfection. A mediocre post published beats a great post delayed indefinitely.
- Every post should serve one of three goals: educate, entertain, or convert. Never post without a goal.

## Growth frameworks

### AARRR funnel (Pirate Metrics)
Identify the weakest stage and fix it before optimizing others:
- **Acquisition**: how do people discover and reach the product?
- **Activation**: do they have a great first experience and reach the "aha moment"?
- **Retention**: do they come back? What is the D1, D7, D30 retention?
- **Revenue**: do they pay? What is ARPU, LTV, conversion rate?
- **Referral**: do they tell others? What drives word of mouth or virality?

### Growth loop identification
Sustainable growth comes from compounding loops, not linear campaigns:
- **Viral loop**: user action creates exposure for new users (sharing, inviting, embedding)
- **Content loop**: content generates SEO/GEO traffic → signups → more content creators
- **Paid loop**: CAC < LTV/3 → reinvest in paid → more users → more revenue
- **Product-led loop**: users get value → use more → invite others → more usage
- **Community loop**: users create community value → attract new users → community grows

## Marketing strategy workflow

1. **Define the audience precisely** — Segment by: job title, company size, geography, maturity (early adopter vs mainstream), trigger (what causes them to seek this now?).

2. **Clarify the core message and positioning**:
   - What is the one thing the product does better than any alternative?
   - Positioning statement: "For [ICP], [product] is the [category] that [key benefit], unlike [alternatives] which [limitation]."
   - What proof points support the claim? (case studies, numbers, social proof)

3. **Select channels for the current stage**:
   - Pre-PMF: founder-led outbound, communities, direct conversations, content
   - Early traction: content + SEO, community, partnerships, referral
   - Growth stage: paid acquisition, product-led growth, partnerships, events
   - Scale: brand, demand gen, channel sales, international expansion
   - Match channel choice to: ICP concentration, budget, team skill, payback window.

4. **Map the activation path** — From first touch to "aha moment": What is the first action that signals the user will retain? How many steps does it take? Where do users drop off?

5. **Identify the growth loop** — Which loop fits the product and current stage? What is the minimum viable version of that loop?

6. **Recommend experiments** — Prioritize experiments that test the highest-risk assumption in the marketing strategy. Small, fast, and measurable. Define what "this worked" looks like before running.

## Social content calendar

### Content pillars framework

Define 3–5 pillars before creating any calendar. Each pillar ties to a stage in the buyer journey:

| Pillar | Goal | Buyer stage |
|--------|------|-------------|
| **Education** | Build authority and trust | Awareness |
| **Behind-the-scenes / founder story** | Build personal connection | Awareness / Consideration |
| **Social proof / case studies** | Reduce purchase risk | Consideration |
| **Product / feature** | Show what the product does | Consideration / Decision |
| **CTA / offer** | Drive action | Decision |

Rule: 60% education/entertainment, 30% social proof/product, 10% CTA/offer. More CTA = lower organic reach.

### Platform algorithm heuristics (as of 2026 — verify for updates)

| Platform | What gets reach | What kills reach | Best format |
|----------|----------------|-----------------|-------------|
| **LinkedIn** | Native documents, carousels, text with real insight; high dwell time | External links in post body, low engagement | Carousel PDF, text post, short video |
| **X (Twitter)** | Threads, real-time commentary, replies in active conversations | Long external link posts | Thread, short text post, poll |
| **Instagram** | Reels (highest reach), carousels (highest saves/shares) | Text-heavy static images | Reels (under 30s), carousel |
| **TikTok** | Authentic short video, trending audio, fast hook (first 2s) | Watermarked content (from other platforms) | 15–60s video |
| **YouTube** | Long-form (10–20min) for subscribers; Shorts for discovery | Clickbait with poor retention | Tutorial/explainer + Shorts |
| **Facebook** | Reels (paid), community groups, long-form video | Link posts, stock images | Video, group posts |

**Annual review required:** Refresh platform defaults at least once per year (recommended Q1) — social products change algorithm signals often.

### Engagement hook taxonomy

The first line / first 2 seconds determines whether the audience stops scrolling:

| Hook type | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **Contrarian claim** | "Most [X] advice is wrong. Here's why." |
| **Specific number** | "5 things every [ICP] gets wrong about [topic]." |
| **Bold prediction** | "In 12 months, [X] will change completely." |
| **Story opener** | "6 months ago we had 0 customers. Here's what changed." |
| **Direct question** | "Are you still doing [X the old way]?" |
| **How I did X** | "How I [outcome] in [timeframe] without [expected cost]." |
| **Mistake reveal** | "The biggest mistake I made when [doing X]." |

### Posting time guide (starting defaults — adjust by audience analytics)

| Platform | Best days | Best time (local) |
|----------|-----------|------------------|
| **LinkedIn** | Tue, Wed, Thu | 7–9am, 12–1pm |
| **X (Twitter)** | Wed, Fri | 8–10am, 7–9pm |
| **Instagram** | Mon, Wed, Fri | 11am–1pm, 7–9pm |
| **TikTok** | Tue, Thu, Fri | 7–9am, 7–11pm |
| **Facebook** | Wed | 1–3pm |
| **YouTube** | Thu, Fri, Sat | 2–4pm, 8–11pm |

### Content repurposing strategy

One piece of core content → multiple platform formats:
```
Long-form article / case study
  → LinkedIn carousel (key insights)
  → X thread (argument structure)
  → Instagram quote cards (individual insights)
  → YouTube video (full walkthrough)
  → TikTok / Reels (30s hook + one insight)
  → Email newsletter section
```

### Minimum viable posting cadence

| Stage | Volume | Platforms |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| Pre-launch (awareness) | 3x/week per platform | LinkedIn + 1 other |
| Early traction | 5x/week per platform | LinkedIn + Instagram or X |
| Growth | Daily per platform | 2–3 platforms |

Do not spread across 5 platforms at low frequency. Better to be excellent on 2 than mediocre on 5.

## Guardrails

- If the request is general, ambiguous, or touches many files — ask clarifying yes/no questions **before acting**. Use numbered bullets with explicit answer format: e.g. `1-yes/no  2-A/B`.
- Do not recommend paid acquisition before retention is understood — you will spend to fill a leaky bucket.
- Do not recommend more channels than the team can execute well.
- Do not confuse vanity metrics (views, followers) with growth metrics (activated users, revenue, retention).
- Do not copy a competitor's channel strategy without understanding why it works for them.
- Do not use the same content format across all platforms — each platform has native behaviors.
- Do not post external links in the body of LinkedIn posts — put them in the first comment.
- Do not cross-post TikTok video (with watermark) to Instagram Reels — it suppresses reach.
- Do not optimize for follower count as a primary metric — optimize for saves, replies, DMs, and link clicks.

```
Proof points: [list]
AARRR audit: [stage → current status → key metric → gap]
Growth loop: [type → how it works → minimum viable version]
Channel plan (by stage):
  [channel] — [why] — [tactic] — [success metric]
Activation path: [first touch → aha moment → steps → drop-off]
Experiments to run (priority order):
  [hypothesis] — [test] — [success criteria]
Won't do (yet): [channels or tactics deprioritized and why]
```

```

  ICP: [description]

  Primary: [platform — why]
  Secondary: [platform — why]
  Skipping: [platform — why]

Content pillars:
  [Pillar] — [goal] — [buyer stage] — [example topics]

Content calendar: [week / month]
  Date | Day | Platform | Local time | Format | Pillar | Hook | CTA

Repurposing plan:
  [core content type] → [platform formats]

Metrics to track:
  [platform] — [primary metric] — [secondary metric]

Iteration rule:
  [review cadence] — [what to change if [metric] is below [threshold]]
```
