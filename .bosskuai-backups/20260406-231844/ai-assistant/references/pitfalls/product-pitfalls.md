# Product pitfalls

Recurring product and UX traps that look fine in demos but break trust, adoption, or clarity.

## Scope and clarity

- **Vague success metrics** — shipping features without a defined “good enough” signal or owner.
- **Everything is P0** — no explicit tradeoff, so quality and predictability collapse.

## User experience

- **Happy path only** — empty, loading, error, and permission-denied states are afterthoughts or generic.
- **Dark patterns** — obscured cancellation, forced continuity, or misleading defaults that create support debt and regulatory risk.

## Onboarding and value

- **Time-to-value too long** — account creation, setup wizards, or mandatory data entry before the user sees benefit.
- **Feature factory** — new surface area without retiring or maintaining old flows; cognitive load grows unchecked.

## Positioning and evidence

- **Claims without proof** — market, performance, or compliance statements that cannot be backed by data or docs.
- **Stale personas** — ICP and jobs-to-be-done never revisited after early assumptions.

## Dependencies and ops

- **Hidden operational load** — manual steps, spreadsheets, or “slack someone” as the real workflow.
- **Single-human bottleneck** — approvals or releases that block on one person with no backup or SLA.
